# Banking AI-Innovator Lab

This example illustrates how the maturity model can be applied to a large bank.

## Context

A bank operates in a highly competitive market. It has large customer segments, transaction data, product usage data, digital channels, branch channels, CRM, marketing analytics, and the ability to run controlled pilots.

The bank wants to discover new products, service models, and category-level moves before committing large budgets.

## Candidate opportunity space

**SME financial resilience**: helping small and medium businesses manage cash flow, obligations, liquidity planning, seasonal volatility, and operational uncertainty.

The strategic shift is from selling isolated financial products to providing an operating layer for financial control and resilience.

## Hypothesis examples

### H1 — Cash-flow resilience cockpit

If SMEs receive a dashboard that combines cash-flow forecast, upcoming obligations, scenario warnings, and recommended actions, then retention and product adoption may improve because the bank becomes a control layer, not only a product provider.

### H2 — Merchant ecosystem card

If merchants receive a bundled proposition combining payments, analytics, rewards, partner offers, and operational insights, then daily engagement may grow because the bank becomes embedded in the merchant workflow.

### H3 — Premium advisory journey

If premium clients receive a proactive financial planning journey with scenario simulation and life-event triggers, then engagement may improve because the bank becomes a decision partner.

## Simulation layers

### Strict model

- revenue;
- cost to serve;
- retention;
- product adoption;
- channel cost;
- operational capacity;
- expected margin;
- payback period.

### Behavioral model

- SME owner trust;
- time pressure;
- fear of complexity;
- willingness to share data;
- sensitivity to fees;
- preference for human vs digital advice;
- reaction to competitor offers.

### Market model

- competitor reaction;
- channel adoption;
- partner ecosystem;
- segment reachability;
- seasonality;
- macro volatility.

## Maturity path

| Step | Maturity level |
|---|---|
| Collect SME pain points and competitor offers | M1 |
| Formulate testable hypotheses | M2 |
| Build financial and operational model | M3 |
| Run scenario distributions | M4 |
| Launch controlled pilots | M5 |
| Explore new category and business model | M6 |

## Example outputs

- p10/p50/p90 impact on retention;
- adoption probability by segment;
- risk of operational overload;
- sensitivity to pricing;
- competitor response scenarios;
- pilot design;
- evidence needed for next model update.

## Why this is enterprise-grade

The bank has enough data and experiment capacity to close the loop. That is what separates an AI-Innovator lab from a strategy brainstorm.
