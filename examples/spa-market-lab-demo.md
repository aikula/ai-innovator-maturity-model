# Service Facility Market Lab Demo Concept

This is a small demo scenario for explaining the AI-Innovator approach in a simple service business.

The commercial focus of the framework is enterprise, but a service facility is useful as a demo because flows, capacity, queues, prices, and customer behavior are easy to understand.

## Object

A small urban service complex with around 50 visitors per day.

Elements:

- reception;
- shared service zone;
- specialized rooms;
- relaxation area;
- premium rooms;
- cafe/bar;
- changing area.

## Goals

### Tactical

- estimate capacity;
- find bottlenecks;
- maximize profit;
- preserve customer satisfaction;
- evaluate price changes;
- test add-on services;
- simulate queues.

### Strategic

- test subscription model;
- test corporate pass;
- test premium quiet hours;
- test evening program;
- test a new category: recovery system rather than one-time visit.

## Customer segments

- busy professionals;
- premium visitors;
- couples;
- price-sensitive visitors;
- regular visitors;
- social visitors;
- corporate clients.

Each segment has:

- goal of visit;
- budget;
- queue tolerance;
- price sensitivity;
- probability of add-on purchase;
- probability of repeat visit;
- satisfaction triggers;
- dissatisfaction triggers.

## Simulation outputs

For each scenario the demo should produce:

- average profit;
- p10/p50/p90 profit;
- average waiting time;
- zone utilization;
- bottlenecks;
- satisfaction score;
- add-on purchases;
- repeat visit probability;
- overload probability;
- recommended microtest.

## Unconventional hypothesis examples

- Night Recovery Club.
- Quiet premium hours.
- Corporate pass.
- Queue-as-ritual.
- No-phone premium zone.
- Couple program.
- Diagnostic funnel.

## Why it matters

The demo is not the main market. It is a sandbox for showing the core loop:

> hypothesis → strict model → behavioral simulation → Monte Carlo → recommended microtest → learning.

If this loop is clear on a small service business, it becomes easier to explain enterprise versions for banks, telecom, retail, and insurance.
