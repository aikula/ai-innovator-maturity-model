## Why this repository exists

AI agents are becoming the default interface for executing work. The next frontier is not only systems that act, but systems that **search for new opportunity spaces**: new products, business models, market categories, pricing strategies, advertising moves, and operating models.

This repository proposes **AI-Innovator Maturity Model v0.1**: a practical framework for distinguishing a basic idea generator from a serious AI system that can:

- collect evidence,
- formulate testable hypotheses,
- combine strict financial/operational models with behavioral simulations,
- run probabilistic scenarios,
- design real-world experiments,
- learn from forecast-vs-actual gaps,
- and explore disruptive business model changes.

The central claim is simple:

> AI-Innovator systems are a game changer only when they close the loop from idea generation to evidence, simulation, experiment, and learning.

Without that loop, they are just chatbots wearing a strategy-consulting costume. Humanity has suffered enough of those.

## Model summary

| Level | Name | Core capability |
|---|---|---|
| M0 | Idea Generator | Produces ideas without evidence or validation |
| M1 | Market Research Assistant | Collects and structures market evidence |
| M2 | Hypothesis Designer | Converts ideas into testable hypotheses |
| M3 | Simulation-Based Innovator | Tests hypotheses through strict operational and financial models |
| M4 | Probabilistic Market Simulator | Runs scenarios repeatedly and reports distributions, not single forecasts |
| M5 | Self-Learning Market Lab | Updates model parameters from real-world experiment results |
| M6 | Business Model Hacker | Searches for new categories, value units, and rules of competition |

## Why large enterprises first

The first practical buyers are likely to be banks, telecoms, large retailers, insurers, industrial companies, energy companies, airlines, healthcare networks, and large real estate/wellness operators.

They have three things smaller companies often lack:

1. **Data**: transactions, CRM, channel analytics, operations, customer behavior, costs.
2. **High-stakes competition**: small improvements or new models can move serious money.
3. **Experiment capacity**: they can run controlled pilots, A/B tests, regional launches, limited offers, and operational simulations.

For them, AI-Innovator is not a toy for brainstorming. It is a way to reduce the cost of strategic mistakes and discover new market moves before committing large budgets.
