# AI-Innovator Maturity Model v0.1 — One-Page Framework

## Definition

AI-Innovator is an AI system that creates testable innovation hypotheses, models their consequences, validates them through simulation and real-world experiments, and improves future strategy from feedback.

## Boundary rule

If it does not validate hypotheses and learn from results, it is not an AI-Innovator.

## Maturity levels

| Level | Name | Output |
|---|---|---|
| M0 | Idea Generator | Ideas |
| M1 | Market Research Assistant | Structured evidence |
| M2 | Hypothesis Designer | Testable hypotheses |
| M3 | Simulation-Based Innovator | Financial and operational scenarios |
| M4 | Probabilistic Market Simulator | Outcome distributions and risks |
| M5 | Self-Learning Market Lab | Updated model and learning memory |
| M6 | Business Model Innovator | New categories, models, and value units |

## Two axes

Depth of innovation: local improvement, product hypothesis, operating model, commercial model, business model, category creation, market redefinition.

Strictness of validation: no validation, critique, evidence, strict model, agent simulation, probabilistic simulation, real-world test, self-learning.

## Architecture

Data Collector, Evidence Store, Market Intelligence, Customer Agents, Competitor Simulator, Strategy Generator, Hypothesis Generator, Critic, Novelty Checker, Operational Simulator, Financial Model, Monte Carlo Runner, Experiment Designer, Real-World Microtests, Learning Memory.

## Enterprise-first thesis

Large enterprises are the first practical buyers because they have data, budgets, competitive pressure, and experiment capacity.

AI-Innovator changes strategic work from opinion-driven planning to evidence-driven simulation and learning.

The promise is not perfect prediction. The promise is cheaper strategic learning before large commitments.
